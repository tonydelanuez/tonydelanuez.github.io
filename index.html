Echocardiogram Design
Washington University in St. Louis

For the final project of a microelectronics class, my colleague Prakrit Srestha and I designed an amplifying circuit that would take the signal from electrodes places at different spots on the human body, and amplify it to a level easily viewable on a computer. The project was a lesson in circuit theory, common-mode voltage filtering, grounding, and proper usage of Wilson's central terminal. We were required to implement the above ideas and more in order to create a design with an acceptable amount of noise and a very clear, stable signal.
